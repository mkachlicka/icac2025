Pitch Perception Task

Participants listened to sequences of tones varying in pitch and detected quiet tones.


Stimuli for the pitch localizer task consisted of several conditions designed to contrast stimuli with a salient pitch with matched acoustic controls. Salient pitch conditions consisted of Missing fundamental (F0) stimuli, sequences of complex tones with 5 adjacent harmonics within the 800 and 3000 Hz range. In the Missing F0 Steady condition, the F0 was set at 200 Hz. In the Missing F0 Changing condition, the F0 was chosen from the set 155.56, 164.81, 174.61, 185, 196, 207.65, 220, 233.08, 246.94, 261.63, 277.18, and 293.66 Hz. F0 sequences were created by starting at 207.65 Hz and then, for each subsequent tone, moving either one step higher or lower in the set of possible F0s, with equal probability of moving up or down.


Two sets of control conditions were designed to match the F0 conditions. Jittered stimuli were created by modifying the missing F0 tones, shifting each harmonic a random amount between -10% and +10% to eliminate the F0 percept (with jitter magnitudes applied consistently throughout a block). Noise stimuli consisted of band-passed Gaussian noise, with the lower boundary of the passband equal to the lowest harmonic in the 5-harmonic set chosen for a particular tone in the sequence, and the upper boundary equal to the highest harmonic.
